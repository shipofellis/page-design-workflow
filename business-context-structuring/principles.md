# Principles for Business Context Structuring

## 1. Context before strategy

This skill prepares the ground for strategy. It should not decide the page narrative, section order, CTA placement, or wireframe. Those decisions belong to later skills.

## 2. Decision-relevant context only

Include context that can influence page design decisions:

- What the product is
- Who the user is
- Why the user cares
- What business outcome matters
- What objections must be handled
- What trust assets are available
- What constraints limit the design
- How success will be measured

Exclude operational details that do not affect the page.

## 3. Explicit uncertainty

Uncertainty is not a weakness. It is a required output.

Use:

- `missing_context` for information that was not provided and may be needed later.
- `assumptions` for reasonable working assumptions that allow the workflow to continue.
- `constraints` for confirmed limitations.

Never hide uncertainty inside polished prose.

## 4. Preserve user language when valuable

If the user provides strong wording about the product, pain point, or value proposition, preserve it. Do not rewrite away nuance too early.

## 5. Separate audience, buyer, and user

For B2B pages, these may differ:

- User: person who uses the product
- Buyer: person who approves purchase
- Evaluator: person comparing options
- Influencer: person recommending the product

Do not collapse them unless the input confirms they are the same.

## 6. Treat competitors broadly

Competitive context includes direct competitors, indirect competitors, manual workflows, internal tools, spreadsheets, agencies, consultants, and doing nothing.

## 7. Success metrics must connect to page goals

If the business goal is waitlist signup, the success metric should not only say “brand awareness.” If the metric is missing, mark it as missing.

## 8. Constraints are first-class inputs

Brand, legal, technical, timeline, localization, accessibility, data availability, and design-system constraints can materially affect page design. Capture them early.
