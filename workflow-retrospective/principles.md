
# Workflow Retrospective Principles

## Principle 1 — Retrospective Is Knowledge Extraction

A retrospective is not a project summary.

It should produce reusable operating knowledge.

---

## Principle 2 — Evidence And Opinion Must Be Separated

A stakeholder preference is not the same as user evidence.

A launch metric is not the same as an internal opinion.

Label evidence quality.

---

## Principle 3 — Reuse Requires Conditions

A pattern is only useful if the team knows:

- when to reuse it
- when not to reuse it
- what inputs it requires
- what risks it carries

---

## Principle 4 — Assumptions Need Lifecycle Management

Assumptions should not disappear after handoff.

They should be:
- validated
- invalidated
- carried forward
- retired

---

## Principle 5 — SOP Updates Should Be Precise

Avoid vague process advice.

Every SOP change should point to:
- affected step
- evidence
- proposed change
- expected benefit

---

## Principle 6 — Failures Are More Valuable Than Smooth Execution

Failures expose missing checks, weak prompts, and unstable assumptions.

---

## Principle 7 — Collaboration Friction Is Process Data

If designers, engineers, or stakeholders were confused, the workflow failed to communicate something.

---

## Principle 8 — The Workflow Should Compound

Each execution should make the next execution faster, clearer, or more reliable.
