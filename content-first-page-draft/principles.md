
# Content-First Principles

## Principle 1 — Structure Before Aesthetics

A beautiful page with weak structure fails.

Content organization determines comprehension.

---

## Principle 2 — Sections Are Functional Units

Every section should:
- reduce uncertainty
- build understanding
- increase trust
- support conversion

Sections are not decorative.

---

## Principle 3 — Copy Supports Strategy

Copy exists to execute:
- positioning
- persuasion
- differentiation
- conversion

Not to sound impressive.

---

## Principle 4 — Narrative Continuity Matters

Sections should feel connected.

A page should read like:
a guided progression

not:
independent content blocks

---

## Principle 5 — Information Density Must Be Intentional

Too little detail creates distrust.

Too much detail creates overload.

---

## Principle 6 — CTA Timing Is Structural

CTAs should appear when:
user readiness increases

not:
whenever empty space exists

---

## Principle 7 — Visual Assets Support Cognition

Visuals should:
- clarify
- demonstrate
- prove
- simplify

Not merely decorate.

---

## Principle 8 — Content Must Survive Redesign

If structure collapses after redesign:
the strategy was weak.
