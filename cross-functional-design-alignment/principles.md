
# Cross-Functional Alignment Principles

## Principle 1 — Critique Is Not Alignment

Critique finds problems. Alignment decides what the team will do.

## Principle 2 — Agreement Must Be Operational

A useful agreement changes execution. If it cannot guide design, engineering, or scope, it is too vague.

## Principle 3 — Tradeoffs Should Be Named

Most design decisions involve tradeoffs between conversion and clarity, ambition and feasibility, brand expression and implementation speed, flexibility and consistency, completeness and cognitive load.

## Principle 4 — Do Not Hide Disagreement

Hidden disagreement becomes late-stage rework. Surface disagreement early and document resolution.

## Principle 5 — Accepted Risk Is Still Risk

If the team accepts a risk, record it. Accepted risk should not disappear from the workflow.

## Principle 6 — Ownership Prevents Drift

Every unresolved item needs an owner. Unowned open questions become process debt.

## Principle 7 — Designers Need Constraints And Freedom

Alignment should clarify what must be preserved, what can be explored, what is not allowed, and what needs further input.

## Principle 8 — Handoff Should Happen After Convergence

The designer handoff brief should represent an aligned plan, not a pile of unresolved comments.
