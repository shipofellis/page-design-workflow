
# Alignment Frameworks

## Critique-To-Decision Framework

For each issue:
1. What is the concern?
2. Who raised it?
3. What decision does it require?
4. What options exist?
5. What tradeoff is involved?
6. What does the team agree to do?
7. What risk is accepted?
8. Who owns follow-up?

## Conflict Classification Framework

### Product vs Design
Conversion pressure conflicts with clarity or brand quality.

### Design vs Engineering
High-quality interaction conflicts with implementation cost.

### Product vs Architecture
Short-term launch need conflicts with long-term platform consistency.

### Growth vs UX
Aggressive capture mechanics conflict with user trust.

### Stakeholder vs Team
Executive preference conflicts with validated user need.

## Decision Status Framework

- Agreed
- Accepted Risk
- Deferred
- Escalated
- Blocked

## Alignment Quality Framework

A strong alignment decision includes:
- specific decision
- rationale
- tradeoff
- affected artifacts
- owner
- risk
- follow-up

## Handoff Readiness Framework

Designer handoff is ready when:
- page mission is not disputed
- section structure is stable
- CTA hierarchy is agreed
- critical technical concerns are bounded
- proof asset gaps have fallback plans
- designer decision space is clear
