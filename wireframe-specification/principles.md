
# Wireframe Principles

## Principle 1 — Layout Is Strategic

Layout determines:
- reading order
- attention flow
- cognitive load
- conversion visibility

It is not decoration.

---

## Principle 2 — Wireframes Validate Thinking

Wireframes should expose:
- structural weakness
- hierarchy confusion
- information overload
- CTA problems

before UI polish hides them.

---

## Principle 3 — Hierarchy Must Exist Without Styling

If a page only works after colors and typography:
the hierarchy is weak.

---

## Principle 4 — Responsive Design Is Structural

Mobile layouts require:
re-prioritization

not:
simple shrinking.

---

## Principle 5 — Density Must Match User Intent

High-intent users tolerate more detail.

Low-intent users require faster scanning.

---

## Principle 6 — CTA Visibility Is Contextual

CTAs should appear when:
user confidence increases

not:
at arbitrary intervals.

---

## Principle 7 — Interactions Must Reduce Friction

Interactions should:
- clarify
- simplify
- reduce overload

Not increase novelty.

---

## Principle 8 — Wireframes Must Survive UI Iteration

The structure should remain valid even if the final visuals change completely.
