
# Orchestration Principles

## Principle 1 — The Workflow Is A State Machine

The workflow is not freeform conversation. Every execution changes workflow state.

## Principle 2 — Steps Exist To Reduce Entropy

Each skill reduces ambiguity in a specific dimension. Skipping steps increases downstream instability.

## Principle 3 — Orchestration Is About Control, Not Generation

The orchestrator manages sequencing, validation, continuity, readiness, and alignment. It does not perform detailed domain execution.

## Principle 4 — Readiness Gates Protect Quality

Premature progression creates weak strategy, bad wireframes, unclear briefs, and expensive redesigns.

## Principle 5 — Critique Must Converge Before Handoff

Multi-role review identifies concerns. Cross-functional alignment decides what to do about them. Handoff should not inherit unresolved disagreement.

## Principle 6 — Assumptions Must Persist

Assumptions are workflow objects. They should survive reviews, alignment, revisions, handoffs, and retrospectives.

## Principle 7 — Rollbacks Are Healthy

A rollback is not failure. It is controlled correction.

## Principle 8 — Artifacts Are System Memory

Artifacts should remain structured, linked, version-aware, and traceable.

## Principle 9 — Stable Inputs Create Stable Outputs

The orchestrator exists to improve determinism across the workflow.
