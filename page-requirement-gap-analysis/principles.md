
# Requirement Gap Analysis Principles

## Principle 1 — Missing Information Is A Product Risk

Requirement gaps are not documentation problems.
They are decision-quality risks.

---

## Principle 2 — Never Hide Uncertainty

If information is missing:
- preserve it
- label it
- estimate risk
- explain impact

Never silently assume.

---

## Principle 3 — Strategy Depends On Information Quality

Weak strategy usually comes from weak inputs.

This skill exists to protect downstream quality.

---

## Principle 4 — Not All Gaps Matter Equally

Prioritize gaps that affect:
- positioning
- conversion
- user psychology
- CTA decisions
- trust
- information hierarchy

Ignore low-impact cosmetic uncertainty.

---

## Principle 5 — Questions Must Be Actionable

Bad:
"Tell me more about users."

Good:
"What is the user's strongest objection before signup?"

---

## Principle 6 — Distinguish Missing vs Contradictory

Missing:
information absent

Contradictory:
multiple inputs conflict

These require different handling.

---

## Principle 7 — Preserve Assumptions Explicitly

Every assumption must include:
- rationale
- risk
- validation need

---

## Principle 8 — Readiness Is Binary

Either:
- safe to continue
or
- blocked

Do not fake confidence.
