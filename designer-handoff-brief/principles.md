
# Designer Handoff Principles

## Principle 1 — A Brief Is A Collaboration Contract

A handoff brief is not a task dump.

It defines shared understanding between PM, design, and implementation stakeholders.

---

## Principle 2 — Preserve Intent, Not Layout Micromanagement

Designers need to understand:
- why the page exists
- what must be communicated
- what user state must change

They do not need every pixel dictated.

---

## Principle 3 — Separate Fixed From Flexible

Confusion happens when everything sounds equally mandatory.

Explicitly separate:
- must preserve
- should consider
- free to explore

---

## Principle 4 — Rationale Is More Useful Than Instructions

Bad:
"Put trust logos under hero."

Better:
"Trust needs to appear early because the user is skeptical about AI reliability."

---

## Principle 5 — Assets Are Part Of The Brief

A page design often fails because required proof assets are missing.

The brief must expose asset gaps early.

---

## Principle 6 — Constraints Should Be Visible

Brand, technical, legal, and timeline constraints should be explicit.

Hidden constraints create rework.

---

## Principle 7 — Design Review Criteria Should Be Defined Before Design

If evaluation criteria are not defined upfront, review becomes subjective.

---

## Principle 8 — The Brief Should Enable Better Design, Not Safer Design

The goal is not to limit the designer.

The goal is to remove ambiguity so the designer can make stronger decisions.
