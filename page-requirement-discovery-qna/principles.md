
# Requirement Discovery Principles

## Principle 1 — Discovery Is Not Brainstorming

The purpose of discovery is not idea generation.

The purpose is reducing uncertainty.

---

## Principle 2 — Questions Must Improve Decisions

Every question should improve:
- strategy quality
- conversion decisions
- positioning clarity
- UX structure
- messaging quality

If not, do not ask it.

---

## Principle 3 — Protect User Attention

Too many questions reduce answer quality.

Ask:
- fewer
- sharper
- higher-leverage questions

---

## Principle 4 — Prioritize Strategic Unknowns

Focus first on:
- user motivation
- objections
- conversion goals
- differentiation
- trust

Not visual preferences.

---

## Principle 5 — Preserve Incomplete Information

If information remains uncertain:
- label it
- preserve it
- estimate risk
- continue carefully

Do not fake completeness.

---

## Principle 6 — Structure Beats Conversation

Discovery outputs should become structured requirement state.

Not long conversational transcripts.

---

## Principle 7 — Every Answer Changes Workflow State

Discovery is stateful.

Every answer should:
- resolve gaps
- create assumptions
- reduce risk
- update readiness

---

## Principle 8 — Readiness Must Be Explicit

The workflow is either:
- ready
- partially ready
- blocked

Avoid ambiguous confidence.
