
# Page Strategy Principles

## Principle 1 — Strategy Before Design

A page should first answer:
why it exists

before:
how it looks

---

## Principle 2 — Pages Are Psychological Systems

A page is not a collection of sections.

It is a progression of user mental states.

---

## Principle 3 — Narrative Drives Structure

Section order should follow persuasion logic.

Not visual symmetry.

---

## Principle 4 — Every Section Needs A Job

Every section must:
- reduce uncertainty
- increase desire
- build trust
- answer objections
- support conversion

Otherwise remove it.

---

## Principle 5 — CTA Timing Matters

Users should not receive:
high-friction CTAs before trust exists.

---

## Principle 6 — Trust Is A System

Trust is not one testimonial.

Trust is cumulative reinforcement across the page.

---

## Principle 7 — Information Hierarchy Is Strategy

Priority communicates importance.

Equal emphasis creates cognitive overload.

---

## Principle 8 — Strategy Must Survive Design Iteration

The strategic logic should remain valid even if UI changes completely.
